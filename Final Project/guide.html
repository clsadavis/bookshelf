History

Shelfmate started out as a humble Google Sheet way back in 2019. As a self-proclaimed bookworm, I enjoyed reading many books across a broad range of genres and topics. The only problem is that I never kept a record of which books I read, and, as many of us have experienced, nowadays it can be difficult to remember what you did the day before, much less what books you read over the course of the year, or, say, over many years.

It upset me to think that all my reading enjoyment (and effort!) was vanishing into the sands of time, so I began tracking my yearly reading on a spreadsheet. But pretty quickly, I found that I needed more and more functionality from my Reading Log, because not only did I want to track the books I had already read, I also wanted to organize the books I wanted to read in the future! And since my TBR sits somehwere around 300 titles any given day, I also needed a way to sort and rank these untried titles, lest I be driven mad or, worse, to indecision when trying to decide what to read next.
